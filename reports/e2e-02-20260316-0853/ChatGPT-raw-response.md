Consistency across environments – Containers package the application together with its dependencies, ensuring it runs the same way on a developer’s machine, in testing, and in production.

Lightweight and fast deployment – Unlike traditional virtual machines, containers share the host OS kernel, making them smaller, faster to start, and more efficient to run.

Isolation and portability – Each container runs in an isolated environment and can be easily moved between systems or cloud platforms without modifying the application.
